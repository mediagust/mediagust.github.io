Digital Advertising Trends of 2020

This is fourth article in digital marketing trends 2020 series. You’ll read about social media & online advertising trends. I hope this guide will help you to define the social media advertising strategy for your products and services. Let’s start.

9. Advertising Automation is Progressing

Over the past 2 years, Facebook has greatly simplified and improved its platform, to the point that current good practices in conversion campaigns are the automation of a maximum of elements.

Optimization of the campaign budget that will be mandatory from February 2020 (according to the latest news). Facebook will automatically split your campaign budget, in real time, within your ad sets (more information in Section 1 of this article). Automatic investments. With its 18 ad placements, Facebook is increasingly encouraging advertisers to keep the "Automatic placements" option so that its algorithm chooses where to run your ads instead.

Dynamic Ads: Facebook automatically shows products in your catalog to the people you target, whether they've already visited your website or not. Dynamic Creative Content: This option allows you to easily test multiple variations of an image/ video, title, snag, link description and call to action (up to 10 images/videos and 5 for other elements). Facebook creates ads, for you, from the resources you provide to it.

All of these 5 advertising tactics based on automation are called "POWER5". Facebook firmly believes that its algorithm is smarter than humans to target your ideal client. As a result, Facebook encourages advertisers to spend less time creating and optimizing their campaigns, but more time thinking about their strategy and creative content. Facebook's obsession with automating the management of your ads is not close to stopping.

Just a few weeks ago, Facebook launched a new feature allowing you to add up to 5 different options for the text, title and description of your ad. You guessed it; Facebook will show different combinations of your texts to users based on what they are most likely to respond to thanks to the data of its algorithm.

Keep in mind that Facebook ad automation is real and will continue to make your life easier. It is even possible that one day Facebook offers you to automatically manage your ads. This day may come, but I remain convinced that digital campaign managers will still have work in 10 years. In any case, I hope. 🙂🙂🙂🙂🙂

10. The Cost of Social Ads May Increase

Facebook and Instagram combined, there are already more than 7 million advertisers (the figures date from the first quarter of 2019). That's a lot compared to the 4 million advertisers in 2016.

What is the relationship with the cost of advertising?

It's (almost) simple. Joseph Donyo (Neomedia) shares in this article a simplified version of Facebook's business model. Facebook's revenue depends on 4 factors:

  • Number of Active Users: The number of people who use Facebook each month.
  • Time Spent: The average time spent by each user on Facebook.
  • Ad Density: The average number of ads each user views on Facebook.
  • Ad Price: The average price paid by advertisers to run an ad on Facebook.

Facebook Instagram user growth is decreasing and the average time spent on Facebook is decreasing (but it's somewhat offset by an increase in the average time spent on Instagram).

So, there is less supply. In addition, the inventory available on some investments (Facebook News Feed) is limited and "already sold". Facebook is starting to run out of space to run ads, who are already everywhere. And as I told you, the number of advertisers is increasing.

So, there is more demand for a limited inventory and a number of users that is not growing as fast. In order for its revenues to increase, the only real growth lever for Facebook is the increase in the price of ads.
