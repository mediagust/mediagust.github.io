What is Digital Marketing About?

Marketing can be defined as the analysis of consumer needs and the set of means of action used by organizations to influence their behaviour. Digital marketing creates value perceived by customers and adapts the business offer of the company to the desires of consumers. In this article you’ll read what is digital marketing about?

Until 2004 it is defined by the 4P, “product policy", "pricing", "placement" and “advertising". After 2004: it is both participatory and social (interacting with consumers via social networks). It affects the entire organization of the company, which is entirely focused on customer satisfaction and no longer on the product.

Digital Marketing Covers Several Fields:

  • Collaborative Marketing
  • Community Marketing
  • Database Marketing
  • Guerrilla Marketing
  • Direct Marketing
  • Mobile Marketing
  • Relationship Marketing
  • Experiential Marketing

Ralph Cordiner, one of General Electric's managers, is often credited in the company's 1952 Annual Report for giving marketing the role it plays today. He wrote: "thus, marketing, through its studies and research, will establish for the engineer and the person in charge of planning and production, what the customer is looking for in a given product, what price he is willing to pay as well as where and when he wants it."

Marketing is an aid to the decision, it allows to define the optimal combination (mix) of the characteristics of the product, able to meet these expectations of consumers, in the most profitable way possible. Finally, it promotes the monitoring of results against the initial objectives.

The Importance of The Customer

"The mission of marketing as a discipline of management of organizations is to build a customer base and ensure, in the long term, its sustained loyalty, by putting day to day and therefore in the very short term, the gestures necessary to strengthen its satisfaction", Christian Dussart and Michel Cloutier specify. By placing the customer at the centre of its reflection, marketing has gradually evolved towards a relational approach whose CRM (Customer relationship management) is supposed to guarantee protection and improvement over time.

"The evolution of marketing is commonly attributed to two phenomena. One would be the multiplication of products meeting the same utility; the other would be a change of the consumer, more unstable in his choice, more individual in his behaviour. The role of marketing can be understood only by inserting this discipline in a dynamic context, only by observing its evolution in parallel with that of the environment," Patrick Gabriel explains. "The traditional doctrine of marketing management recognizes customer orientation as the hard core of marketing since it leads to a double positive result: customer satisfaction and company performance" recalls Gilles Marion.

Marketing today is declining in various areas of specialization, which sometimes undermines the qualities of the original concept. "Digital Marketing therefore represents the function of the company's relationship with its markets by creating the most acceptable forms of satisfaction. To ensure the "spearhead" function that is its own, by respecting the criteria of profitability required by general policy, marketing manifests itself as a system: research, strategy, action. These three operations are mutually supportive and Interactive," said Jacques Antoine and Guy Serraf.

To conclude it we can say – digital marketing is about to build a customer base and ensure the long-term engagement through various digital marketing methods and tools.
