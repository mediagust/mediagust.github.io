Search Engine Optimization (SEO) Trends of 2020

11. Search Results Are Changing and Your Organic Traffic May Decrease

Today, more than ever, you want answers right away. You no longer want to do a Google search, explore 3 sites and read 8 articles before finding your answer. Maybe it was revolutionary 10 years ago. It's not now.

Google wants to provide the answers to your questions right away so that you constantly come back to its site to do research. Google becomes a response engine, and less a search engine. This is the trend emerging.

As the moderator's blog explains very well in this article, "Google, through the knowledge graph, featured snippets and other structured data, is increasingly becoming a response engine. Google's goal is not to direct traffic to the most relevant sites, its goal is to directly serve the response to the user."

As a result, search results (SERP) push you to less click on Search links. There are more and more "featured snippets" that give you an answer right away. The "other questions asked" feature, introduced in 2018, is gaining importance.

All these ads and features push organic results down. Neil Patel compared the search results for a given keyword between 2015 and 2019. The difference is obvious. The first (organic) search result is always lower on the page.

If everything I've just told you still doesn't speak to you, understand that it will inevitably be harder to get organic traffic from Google because Google seeks to directly provide answers to your questions including through featured snippets and other search features. A study conducted by Rand Fishkin (SparkToro) using data collected by Jumpshot proves this: we click less and less on Google search results.

More than a billion requests on Google US were analyzed and 50.33% of them received no clicks (this study dates from June 2019), which means that you will get a response directly on the Google search results page (SERP) without having to click on a link and visit a website. For example, to the question "how much pasta per person," Google gives me an "acceptable" answer thanks to the featured snippets and the other questions asked.

If I'm looking to find out where Brussels is, Google serves me the answer on a silver platter by showing me Brussels on the map. In short, to adapt to the decline in organic traffic, you will need to take a multi-channel approach (detailed in point 15 of this article) and seek to position yourself on long-lagging keywords.

12. More Importance to Optimization for Voice SEO

Today, I am sure that you perfectly master the written search on Google. For example, if you wanted to learn more about digital marketing trends, you would do such a search: "digital marketing trends”. This is changing with voice search on mobile.

By 2020, ComScore estimates that 50% of searches will be voice. By the way, if you want to do the test, use the Google app on your smartphone and do a voice search. Voice assistant will then give the answer to your question, without having to visit the website in question.

Do not believe that voice assistants are gadgets of the future. According to a recent Microsoft study, 72% of internet users use voice search via voice assistants in their everyday lives. So, voice SEO has a future! It is even present in some parts of the world such as the USA.

What we have to understand is that these searches performed by voice are not quite the same as those entered on the keyboard. Rather than writing multiple keywords without linking them with determinants or prepositions, the searches they did by voice are longer and are comparable to whole sentences (questions or affirmations).

For example, instead of writing "bakery 13th arrondissement" with your keyboard on Google, you will ask Google "where to find a bakery in the 13th arrondissement”? As you might suspect, voice search has an impact on the SEO of your content since it will be necessary to optimize them to show the algorithm that they have the answer to voice searches.

Among these optimizations, SEO professionals advise to work on long-lagging keywords, including interrogative adverbs (who, how, where, why, etc.), but also to give simple and short answers. Ideally, Google will be able to retrieve your answers and place them in a featured snippet (as we saw in the previous point).

Since these searches are usually done using your phone or a voice assistant, voice SEO also "requires" you to have a "mobile-friendly" site, Fast (Google does not risk waiting for your site to load to find the answer to your question) and secure with the HTTPS protocol.
