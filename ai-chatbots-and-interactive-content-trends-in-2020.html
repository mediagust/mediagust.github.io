AI, Chatbots and Interactive Content Trends in 2020

13. Artificial Intelligence

For some years now, artificial intelligence (AI) has been part of our lives. The example I always have in mind when I think about AI is Siri which has been around since 2011. Today, Siri is even able to remind you of important phone calls to pass.

Artificial intelligence is not something very human and one can understand that some are reluctant to do so. However, we will see that chatbots (conversational robots) are rather well adopted by internet users.

You already know them:
  • Apple Siri
  • Google Assistant
  • Amazon's Alexa
  • Microsoft Cortona

They have nothing human, but they're damn smart!

Let's Take Alexa.

Alexa, it's kind of your assistant to whom you can ask all kinds of things and who does the research for you. For example, if you want to go on vacation in Ibiza, you can ask Alexa:

  • What are the best hotels?
  • What weather it will be next week?
  • What are the best places to visit?

This technology is already well adopted in the USA. To learn a little more about Alexa, I recommend this video by Gary Vaynerchuk who claims that Alexa is the new voice search engine, and future competitor of Google.

In the coming years, expect to see companies integrate Alexa and voice search into their digital strategy.

The Chatbots

Those robots that make you the conversation on Messenger. You think it's intrusive?

A study conducted by LivePerson (5000 respondents) shows that respondents are rather positive about their experience with a chatbot. 38% of respondents had a positive experience compared to only 11% who had a negative experience.

In your opinion, what differentiates chatbots from customer service the most?

Their availability?

They are available 24/7/365! They don't sleep. This means that when you are not logged in, they can offer a response to a client request.

This is not their only use. Among the most common uses of chatbots are:

  • Get a quick response in an emergency
  • Get detailed answers or explanations
  • Pay an invoice
  • Buy a product
  • Join an email list
  • And many more.

Domino's Pizza is the example I most like to refer to present a profitable use of chatbots. In the USA, if you click "send a message”, you will be introduced to a chatbot that will invite you to:

  • Make an order
  • Redo an order placed
  • Follow an order

You understand, you can order a pizza without interacting with a person on the phone, amazing!

I found you another example. This time, a conversation between a user and the Rayban brand bot. While this seems complex to set up, there are inexpensive software that allows you to set up a chatbot, such as ManyChat or MobileMonkey. You can also set up scenarios on Messenger, setting up automatic answers to common questions or queries. Expect to see more companies using chatbots in 2020 to improve their customer service.

Artificial intelligence also opens the door to the personalization of content, offers and advertisements. Among the known examples of customization, we find the home page customized according to your past visits and the products you have searched for on an e-commerce site.

I'm thinking Amazon or Zalando. Amazon also likes to make recommendations based on your past research. Consumers themselves expect to find a personalized home page or relevant offers compared to what they have already seen on your site.

14. Interactive Content (again and again)

Digital marketing is no longer just writing blog articles for SEO and waiting for traffic to arrive. The Internet is interactive now. Think of live chat on e-commerce sites. You are going to tell me that a post on social networks is interactive content and that it has existed for years already.

True, these publications create engagement (comments, shares, reactions), but it seems to be less effective than before. To differentiate, brands and companies need to adopt new, more interactive forms of content.

To generate leads, you could use guides or white papers like I do. But you can do better. You simply answer the questions and to know your results, you will need to give your email to receive them, clever! There are also quizzes on e-commerce sites. This allows you to choose for yourself the product that best suits your situation according to your answers to the quiz.

Live Streaming

Live videos are another form of interactive content. According to Facebook, users spend 3 times more time watching Lives and comment 10 times more on a Live than a normal video. Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, YouTube and so on. today, you can go live on virtually any social platform: Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, YouTube, etc… And you can do it simultaneously. Neil Patel advises to make several live videos simultaneously on several platforms. That way, you touch your audience where it is at the moment. Be careful, this strategy requires means since you must have several cameras and one or more moderators to spot and respond to comments from viewers.

Survey

They are very effective at engaging your audience in certain decisions of your company or collecting feedback. For example, if you have just written a book, you could offer two book covers to your audience and ask them which one you should choose. On Facebook last year, they landed in the pre-recorded videos and live videos on Facebook.

Polls in Stories

Instagram Stories are also a type of content that elicits quite a lot of reactions. You can also add polls to spice up your Stories, but you already know it! In 2018 and 2019, Instagram introduced the “Questions", "Discussion" and "Quiz" Stickers to further strengthen the relationship you can develop with your audience.

Polls in Ads

Facebook and Instagram ads also landed in the polls in 2019. They were initially available only for ads in Instagram Stories. They are also in the ads of the Facebook News Feed (single image format and video). The proof that Facebook wants (also) to make its ads more interactive, well aware of the fact that users can no longer stop clicking on everything that moves.

Augmented Reality Ads

A year ago, the news fell: Facebook is testing augmented reality ads on a small group of advertisers. You will soon be able to virtually try a lipstick or sunglasses through your phone's camera. Another example of "mobile-first" and interactive Advertising. However, Facebook has not yet communicated on the global deployment of this advertising format, but this should not be later.

15. The Number of Touch Points Before Conversion Will Increase

Bravo, you read so far. I'm sure your brain is bubbling and you're saying to yourself: where to start? The reality is that in 2020, you can no longer depend on a single marketing channel as it was 5 years ago. I'll explain why.

In this article, I told you about many platforms or content that can be integrated into your digital strategy, such as:

  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • LinkedIn
  • Twitter
  • YouTube
  • Pinterest
  • Google (SEO)
  • IGTV
  • The micro-content
  • Social ads
  • Video

Today, it is no longer enough to be present on a single platform, it is more interesting to integrate several of them into your strategy. On the Internet, it is said that it takes on average 7 points of contact before a perfect stranger becomes a customer in your home.

Today, you probably need twice as much, so you are active on multiple media / platforms at the same time. Your attention is becoming more and more difficult to get and it will have to be done. You can't depend on a single platform to get these 15 points of contact. It's possible of course, but it's risky (you have no control over the algorithms of Google, Facebook and the rest).

To succeed, you need to combine (or integrate) multiple channels or media together in your digital strategy. You have no choice but to reuse your content and adapt it to each platform on which you distribute it.
