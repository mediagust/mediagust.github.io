All About Inbound Marketing

Inbound marketing literally means "inbound marketing‚Äù: this digital marketing strategy is based on the idea of attracting natural traffic through quality content rather than going out to your potential prospects.

The methodology is based on 4 pillars: attract, convert, transform and retain.

How Does Inbound Marketing Generate More Leads?

The regular issue of B2B SaaS often boils down to this question: How to find new leads?

With the new consumption habits, consumers no longer wait for brands to come for them, they go directly to them: Inbound marketing was born from this observation.

Companies have understood this and are gradually turning away from this somewhat forward-looking marketing: according to a HubSpot study, in 2016, some 17% of marketers still believe that outbound would be more effective for lead generation.

The obvious solution, instead of looking for leads on the Internet, is to use the Internet to naturally attract leads, thanks to tools like:

  • The website: showcase of your activity on the web, essential to provide the information sought by the customer;
  • Creating a blog: to give an informative added value to your prospect;
  • The use of social networks: to expand your audience and generate maximum leads;

The role of marketers: instead of prospecting, marketers are turning to a base of already interested prospects through inbound marketing; their role is now to convert them!

The success of inbound marketing in numbers:
  • 92.34% of companies say they increase their traffic;
  • 92.7% generate more leads;
  • 42.2% increase their conversion rate from leads to customers.

Moreover, the HubSpot solution is positioned as the world leader among inbound marketing platforms. Indeed, it is an all-in-one tool that brings together the features of CRM, CMS, social publications etc...

Content Marketing Versus Inbound Marketing?

Are content marketing and inbound marketing two opposing strategies or vice versa closely related?

The content is sort of the fuel of your inbound marketing strategy: so the two are rather related.

If you are developing an Inbound marketing strategy, content is a key component. Inbound works with relevant content, which stems from a strong content strategy: your blog posts, for example, need to resonate with each other to provide a precise approach to your information sector and thus convert your leads. Content and inbound marketing go hand in hand: published articles need the support of an overall inbound campaign to reveal their full potential and achieve optimal reach.

Ultimately, an ideal digital marketing strategy would be to use every existing lever. But for lack of resources and at the risk of spreading, this is not always possible.

Focus on the needs of your business, the nature of your prospects (B2B or B2C), the timing imposed on you, your technical and financial means to design a personalized strategy.
