Strategy of Digital Marketing in 2020

For decades, the golden rule of any good marketer was to pick up the customer. Digital marketing has obviously not escaped the trend, and companies have saturated the web with advertising messages, addressed to as many in the hope of reaching a few. However, this strategy of digital marketing proves to be rather ineffective today: saturated with ads, the web sees its users tired of this permanent beating.

Here are some 2020's effective strategies of digital marketing.

Social Media Strategy

Social media or SMO (social media optimization) is an integral part of any effective digital marketing. Understand by SMO the expanded field of searches through social networks, increasingly in vogue with the use of hashtags for example.

Social networks allow, among other things, to increase traffic to your site through the publication of regular posts about your activity. Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter have become a must-see in ten years, they are equally well used by B2B professionals and SaaS publishers.

What is Social Media Strategy For 2020?
  • Increase the visibility of your SaaS B2B business: by publishing content regularly, you will expand your audience and the visibility of your business.
  • Improve communication with customers: every post, like, share, private message used by internet users is a communication channel.
  • Reduce marketing costs: cheaper than an ad campaign, a well-built social media strategy can be just as profitable.
  • Deepen the knowledge of customers / prospects: the information collected allows to refine the knowledge of your personas and therefore of your offer.
  • Generating trust and credibility: the presence of a SaaS company on social networks generates trust and implies an ability to respond and deal with problems effectively.

Content Marketing: The Center Pillar of Digital Marketing

Content marketing, is an essential component of digital marketing and in particular of inbound marketing. The content marketing is simply the provision of informative content, fun and with real added value in order to seduce its customers or prospects. White papers, digital magazines, blogs dedicated to brand image: there are many ways to nurture such a strategy.

Content Marketing Serves To:
  • Generate visits;
  • Promote conversion in e-commerce: videos, customer reviews, size advice, etc;
  • Create a support dedicated to the valorization of the brand.

Native Advertising Within Your Digital Marketing Strategy

Native advertising is a form of advertising that has been created with or against the massive arrival of advertising banners on the web. The idea is to produce an advertisement that does not look like an advertisement.

By integrating naturally on the website of a media, in the form of "brand content", native advertising integrates with already existing content and appears as an additional source of information.

Native advertising can take a wide variety of forms:
  • Sponsored business links that appear in search results;
  • Sponsored tickets: sponsored content that you see regularly on social networks;
  • Recommendation Widgets: broadcast on quality sites;
  • Sponsored lists: on Google, Amazon, Etsy, etc…
  • Background articles or infographics: articles or infographics that highlight the company's product or service in a more general context, published in the web press.

Companies are increasingly calling for this in the face of the increase in adblockers and the enhancement of content on the Internet.

Digital Marketing and Online Public Relations

Public relation is an important strategy in the broad field of digital marketing. They aim to develop your company's image on the Internet, namely it’s e-reputation.

In fact, a PR campaign asserts your credibility on the web: if the Internet talks about you through different channels, you stand out. Online communities and blogs are the main supports to develop your public relations and share your company's values.

The Importance of E-Mail Marketing

E-mailing refers to marketing campaigns broadcast in the form of e-mails, with the purpose of promoting a company, product or service by addressing a user directly through his or her e-mail address. For a long time, king of acquisition strategies, however, e-mailing suffered from automatic blockers of mailboxes that sent them right away in spam.

Did the e-mail die for that? Well, No! An e-mail campaign would even perform better than a sponsored campaign on social networks. Even today, e-mail offers a very direct form of contact with your customers and prospects in B2B.

For an effective B2B email campaign, here are some tips:
  • Take care of the object of your mail: it must be impactful to push the recipient to open it;
  • Use good software and segment the database;
  • Give preference to a short, legible message and a neat design;
  • Be respectful of regulations: beware of purchased e-mail lists for which you do not have the consent of your contacts;
  • Clearly express the contribution of your added value to the client / prospect.

Think About Mobile Marketing in Your Digital Marketing Strategy

The invasion of smartphones in our daily lives has led to the emergence of a new form of mobile marketing. The figures are not misleading: in 2018, Global Internet traffic via computer accounts for only 46%, compared to 54% for mobile devices.

By 2021, mobiles are expected to reach 61% of global internet traffic.

Faced with such a preponderance, some companies have finally developed a "mobile first" strategy: that is, the bulk of the efforts and investments are aimed at mobile users.

To establish a "mobile first" strategy, the methods vary from responsive design to the creation of mobile applications.

Affiliate Marketing

Affiliation is a contract between a merchant site and partner sites affiliated to its program. Concretely, it means that a partner site hosts affiliate content (banners, links, product catalogs) that will redirect the visitor to the merchant site. This marketing method helps to increase traffic acquisition.

The affiliate offers a win-win relationship:
  • If you are the merchant site, you increase your traffic, while the partner site receives a commission on every click or sale coming from its site;
  • Commissions are variable or fixed on sales or clicks generated.

The Importance of Marketing Automation

An automated marketing that would do everything in your place? Not exactly, but almost! This method allows you to automate certain tasks, analyze the collected data and then customize your communication.

How Does Marketing Automation Work?

Marketing automation gives the opportunity to initially study the behavior of leads or customers, by delighting their behaviors in front of the different options offered.

Marketing automation analysis:
  • Web browsing;
  • Reactions related to e-mailing: read time, click rate, etc.;
  • Behavior on social networks: engagement, number of likes, shares, comments, etc.

In a second step, it allows you to adapt your inbound marketing strategy according to automatically generated statistics: to give a concrete example of the results of marketing automation, 80% of leads rejected by sales people become customers within 24 months.

How is that possible?

Marketing automation acts on several fronts:

  • It refines the scoring;
  • It adapts storytelling to the lead/client;

A marketing automation software is complementary to an inbound marketing strategy: it takes over where the inbound strategy is less efficient, and intensifies the results of your strategy implemented.

If you are developing an effective strategy of digital marketing, content is a key component. Digital marketing works with relevant content, which stems from a strong content strategy: your blog posts, for example, need to resonate with each other to provide a precise approach to your information sector and thus convert your leads.
